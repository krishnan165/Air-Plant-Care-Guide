Air Plant Care Guide

Soil for Air Plant

No soil required.

Air Plants, or Tillandsia, don't need soil. They absorb nutrients and moisture through their leaves from the air.

Watering

Soak or mist regularly.

Soak Air Plants in water for about 20-30 minutes every 1-2 weeks or mist them 2-3 times a week, especially in dry environments.

Lighting

Indirect, bright light.

Place Air Plants in locations with indirect, bright light. They can tolerate some direct sunlight but avoid prolonged exposure.

Benefits

No soil mess.

Air Plants are mess-free as they don't require soil, making them a unique and easy-to-care-for plant option.

Adaptable and versatile.

These plants can be displayed in various creative ways, such as hanging or in decorative containers, adding a touch of nature to any space.

Non-Benefits

Sensitive to extreme cold; protect from freezing temperatures. Overwatering can lead to rot, so ensure proper drying after watering.
